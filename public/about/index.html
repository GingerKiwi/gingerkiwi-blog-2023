Also starting when I was still in university I spent 18 years self-employed in special education as an academic coach and special education tutor. I taught organizational and study skills along with subject specific skills (especially maths and sciences) to students with dyslexia, adhd, anxiety, depression, and learning disabilities. I acted as the go between for families, students and school staff by helping families navigate the paperwork and bureaucracy of special needs supports and regulations.

During the final seven years I expanded my skills into becoming an Adaptive Educational Technology Specialist. Technology is a game changer for many people with disabilities and neurodiversity. Customizing iPad, MacBook, and even mobile phones for students means they could focus on learning and not on trying to write or read. Yes, I taught writing and reading skills too - but when the goal is to learn chemistry for a course that can be met by listening as well as reading. Finding solutions for students with complex learning needs was a challenging puzzle.
